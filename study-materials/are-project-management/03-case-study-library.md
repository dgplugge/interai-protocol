# Case Study 3: Municipal Library Branch

## Scenario Narrative

**CivicSpace Architects**, a 10-person firm focused on public and institutional
projects, has been selected by the **City of Glenbrook** to design a new branch
library. The project, the **"Glenbrook East Branch Library,"** includes:

- **Single-story building**: 9,600 SF on a 0.8-acre municipal lot
- **Program spaces**: Adult collection, children's area, teen zone, 2 meeting rooms,
  maker space, staff workroom, public restrooms
- **Site work**: 28-space parking lot, accessible entry plaza, stormwater rain garden,
  book drop lane
- **Sustainability**: The City requires the building to meet **LEED Silver** certification
  (minimum) — the City's capital improvement policy mandates LEED Silver for all new
  municipal buildings over 5,000 SF

**Key Team Members at CivicSpace:**
- **Denise Okafor, AIA, LEED AP** — Principal-in-Charge
- **James Thorne** — Project Manager (your role for this case study)
- **Elena Vasquez** — Project Architect / Interior Designer

**Consultants:**
- **Atlas Engineering** — Structural
- **ClearPoint MEP** — Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing
- **SiteLogic Civil** — Civil engineering and landscape
- **EcoMetrics** — LEED consulting / energy modeling

**Contractor:**
- **Daniels Municipal Construction** — General Contractor (selected via **competitive
  sealed bid** per city procurement code; lowest responsible bidder)

### Project Budget
- **Total project budget**: $4,800,000 (funded by municipal bond referendum)
- **Construction budget**: $3,900,000
- **Soft costs**: $900,000 (design fees, permits, FF&E, technology, contingency)
- **Construction contingency**: $195,000 (5% of construction budget)
- **City policy**: Any change order exceeding $25,000 requires **City Council approval**
  at a public meeting (meetings held 1st and 3rd Tuesday of each month)

### Project Timeline
- **Design start**: September 2, 2024
- **SD completion**: November 15, 2024
- **DD completion**: February 14, 2025
- **CD completion**: May 16, 2025
- **Public bid opening**: June 24, 2025
- **Contract award**: July 8, 2025
- **Construction start**: August 4, 2025
- **Substantial Completion target**: May 22, 2026
- **Grand opening target**: July 4, 2026 (City's 150th anniversary celebration)
- **Total construction duration**: 42 weeks

### Current Situation (as of the date of this case study)
It is **November 2025** — roughly 13 weeks into construction. The project has been
proceeding on schedule, but several issues have emerged:

1. **Value engineering dispute**: During bidding, the low bid came in at **$4,180,000**
   — $280,000 over the $3,900,000 construction budget. After negotiation, a value
   engineering (VE) package reduced the contract to **$3,920,000** (only $20,000 over
   budget, absorbed from contingency). However, some VE substitutions are now causing
   quality concerns.

2. **Submittals backlog**: The contractor has submitted 40 of 68 required submittals,
   but **18 are still pending Architect review**. The contractor claims the review
   backlog is delaying procurement and will cause schedule delays. The Architect's
   contract requires review within **10 business days** of receipt.

3. **Owner-requested addition**: The Library Director has asked to add a **drive-through
   book return lane** with an automated sorting system. This was removed during VE
   but the Library Board has since allocated an additional $85,000 from their operating
   budget for this feature.

4. **LEED documentation gap**: EcoMetrics has flagged that two VE substitutions (roofing
   membrane and interior paint) may not meet the specified environmental product
   declaration (EPD) requirements for the LEED Materials & Resources credits. If these
   credits are lost, the project drops below LEED Silver threshold.

### EcoMetrics Memo — November 5, 2025

**TO:** James Thorne, CivicSpace Architects
**FROM:** Nadia Kowalski, EcoMetrics
**RE:** LEED Materials & Resources Credit Risk — VE Items 01 and 02

James —

During our LEED documentation review, we identified that two value engineering
substitutions are jeopardizing 4 points in the Materials & Resources (MR) category:

**MR Credit: Building Product Disclosure and Optimization — EPD (2 points)**
- Requires a minimum of 20 permanently installed products with Environmental Product
  Declarations (EPDs)
- Original design specified 23 products with EPDs
- VE-01 (roofing membrane) and VE-02 (interior paint) both removed EPD-certified products
- Current count: **21 EPD-certified products**... however, Submittals 07-01 and 09-01
  were returned "Revise & Resubmit" because the submitted products lack EPDs
- If the contractor installs the non-EPD products per the VE specification, count drops
  to **19 — below the 20-product threshold**

**MR Credit: Building Product Disclosure and Optimization — Material Ingredients (2 points)**
- Requires Health Product Declarations (HPDs) for 20 products
- VE-02 (paint) substitution removed HPD+EPD certified paint but retained HPD-only
- This credit is currently at 20 products — **no margin for error**

**Options:**
1. **Revert VE-01 and VE-02** to original EPD-certified products (+$42,000 cost)
2. **Find alternative EPD-certified products** at similar cost to the VE substitutes
   (requires contractor cooperation and resubmittal)
3. **Accept the point loss** and attempt to make up 4 points elsewhere (difficult at
   this stage — most other credits are already maximized or require design changes)

**Recommendation:** Option 2 is preferred. I've identified two potential substitute
products:
- Roofing: GenFlex EZ TPO 60-mil — EPD-certified, cost delta approximately +$8,000
  vs. VE substitute
- Paint: Sherwin-Williams Harmony — EPD + HPD certified, cost delta approximately
  +$3,000 vs. VE substitute

Total additional cost for Option 2: approximately **$11,000** — significantly less than
reverting to original specs.

This needs to be resolved before the products are ordered. The roofing membrane has a
4-week lead time and is scheduled for installation starting January 12, 2026.

Please advise.

—Nadia
