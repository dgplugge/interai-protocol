# Case Study 2: Historic Courthouse Adaptive Reuse

## Scenario Narrative

**Preservation Design Associates (PDA)**, a 15-person firm specializing in historic
preservation and adaptive reuse, has been engaged by **Whitfield County** to renovate
and repurpose the **1928 Whitfield County Courthouse** into a mixed-use civic building.
The project, known as **"The Whitfield Center,"** includes:

- **Ground floor**: County clerk's office (retained) + new public café and gallery space
- **Second floor**: Conversion of former courtrooms into co-working / incubator suites
- **Third floor**: County administrative offices (modernized)
- **Basement**: New mechanical plant, IT infrastructure, and archive storage
- **Exterior**: Restoration of limestone facade, windows, and terra cotta ornament

The building is listed on the **National Register of Historic Places** and is located
within a **local historic district**, meaning all exterior modifications require approval
from the **Historic Preservation Commission (HPC)**.

**Key Team Members at PDA:**
- **Alan Whitford, FAIA** — Principal / Preservation Specialist
- **Rachel Dunn** — Project Manager (your role for this case study)
- **Carlos Medina** — Project Architect
- **One preservation technician** — Field documentation

**Consultants:**
- **Heritage Structural** — Structural engineering (specializing in historic masonry)
- **Apex MEP** — Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing
- **Conservator Inc.** — Material conservation (stone, terra cotta)
- **Code Red Consulting** — Code and accessibility compliance

**Contractor:**
- **Landmark Builders, Inc.** — General Contractor (selected via qualifications-based

### Project Timeline
- **Design start**: April 1, 2024
- **HPC Design Review (exterior)**: August 12, 2024 — Approved with conditions
- **SD completion**: June 15, 2024
- **DD completion**: October 30, 2024
- **CD completion**: March 7, 2025
- **Permit issuance**: April 14, 2025
- **Construction start**: May 5, 2025
- **Substantial Completion target**: May 1, 2026
- **Total construction duration**: 52 weeks

### Project Budget
- **Construction budget**: $8,400,000
- **Funded by**: County capital bond ($6,000,000) + State Historic Tax Credit ($1,500,000)
  + Federal Historic Tax Credit ($900,000)
- **Tax credit compliance** is administered through the **State Historic Preservation
  Office (SHPO)** and requires adherence to the **Secretary of the Interior's Standards
  for Rehabilitation**

### Current Situation (as of the date of this case study)
It is **September 2025** — roughly 18 weeks into construction. Three significant issues
have emerged:

1. **Concealed structural damage**: During selective demolition of the second-floor
   courtroom partitions, the contractor discovered extensive **termite damage and wood
   rot** in the structural timber floor framing. The original construction documents were
   based on visual inspection only — destructive investigation was limited by the
   county's desire to minimize damage before construction. Heritage Structural estimates
   that **40% of the second-floor timber joists require sistering or replacement**.

2. **HPC conflict**: The exterior window restoration is revealing that **12 of 47
   original wood windows** are deteriorated beyond repair. The HPC-approved design
   called for restoration of all original windows. Replacing these 12 windows with
   historically compatible replicas requires **returning to the HPC for amended approval**,
   which meets monthly and has a 30-day submission deadline.

3. **Tax credit risk**: The SHPO reviewer has flagged a concern that the proposed
   co-working buildout on the second floor may not meet the Secretary of the Interior's
   Standards because the new partition layout "obscures the historic character of the
   original courtroom volume." The $2.4M in combined tax credits could be at risk.

## Supporting Document B: Contractor's Change Proposal — Structural Remediation

### Landmark Builders, Inc.
### Proposed Change Order — PCO-004
**Date:** September 8, 2025
**Project:** Whitfield Center — Historic Courthouse Renovation
**Reference:** RFI-023, RFI-024, Supplemental Structural Report dated August 29, 2025

---

**Description of Changed Condition:**
During selective demolition of non-historic partitions on the second floor (as directed
by CD drawing A-201, Demolition Plan), concealed structural timber framing was exposed
revealing:

- Extensive termite damage to floor joists in Bays C through G (approximately 40% of
  second-floor framing)
- Wood rot at joist pockets where timbers bear on exterior masonry walls (moisture
  infiltration over decades)
- Evidence of prior undocumented repair patches (non-original lumber) in Bays D and E

**Heritage Structural's supplemental report (August 29, 2025)** recommends:
- Full sistering of 18 joists with engineered lumber
- Replacement of 6 joists that are structurally compromised beyond repair
- New steel bearing plates at 12 joist pocket locations
- Temporary shoring of second floor during all remediation work

---

**Cost Breakdown:**

|---|---|---|---|

---

**Schedule Impact:**
- Remediation work will require **6-8 weeks** on the second floor
- Temporary shoring must be in place before any work above can proceed
- Second-floor co-working buildout delayed by corresponding duration
- **Projected schedule impact: 4 weeks added to Substantial Completion**
  (some overlap possible with other floor work)
- **New projected Substantial Completion: May 29, 2026**

---

**Contractor's Position:**
This constitutes a **concealed condition** as defined in AIA A201-2017 § 3.7.4.
The condition was not visible or reasonably discoverable during the design phase.
The Owner accepted the risk of limited pre-construction investigation (per
Owner-Architect Agreement § 7.3). We request approval to proceed under a
**Construction Change Directive** while the formal Change Order is negotiated,
to avoid further schedule delays.

## Supporting Document C: SHPO Review Letter + Internal Memo

### Document C-1: SHPO Review Letter

**STATE HISTORIC PRESERVATION OFFICE**
**Division of Historical Resources**

**Date:** August 22, 2025
**Re:** Whitfield County Courthouse — Part 2 Application Review
**Project Number:** HP-2024-0847
**Tax Credit Type:** Federal 20% + State 25% Historic Rehabilitation Tax Credit

Dear Mr. Whitford:

We have reviewed the Part 2 application for the above-referenced project and offer the
following preliminary comments:

**APPROVED ELEMENTS:**
- Exterior limestone facade restoration approach
- Window restoration methodology (for restorable units)
- Basement mechanical conversion
- Ground-floor program (clerk's office retention, café/gallery use)
- Third-floor administrative office modernization

**ELEMENT REQUIRING FURTHER REVIEW:**
- **Second-floor co-working / incubator buildout**: The proposed partition layout on
  Drawing A-302 creates 14 enclosed offices within the two former courtrooms (Rooms 201
  and 204). The original courtrooms featured double-height volumes with decorative
  plaster ceilings at 18 feet. The proposed partitions extend to 9 feet, leaving the
  upper volume exposed, but the SHPO is concerned that the **density of partitions
  obscures the spatial character** of these historically significant rooms.

**RECOMMENDATION:**
The SHPO recommends the applicant consider:
1. Reducing the number of enclosed offices to 8 or fewer
2. Using glass partitions above 42 inches to maintain visual transparency
3. Ensuring that the decorative plaster ceilings remain fully visible from the primary
   circulation path

**If the current design is submitted without modification, the SHPO may issue a
"conditional" Part 2 approval**, which could delay or complicate the tax credit
certification process. A "not consistent" determination would jeopardize the
$2,400,000 in combined tax credits.

We are available for a pre-submission consultation at your convenience.

Sincerely,
Margaret Liu, AIA
State Historic Preservation Officer

  changes simultaneously to avoid double-work
