# Case Study 1: Riverfront Mixed-Use Development

## Scenario Narrative

**Meridian Architecture**, a 25-person firm in a mid-sized Midwestern city, has been
engaged by **Lakeshore Development Group (LDG)** to design a 6-story mixed-use building
on a former industrial waterfront parcel. The project, known as **"The Confluence,"**
includes:

- **Ground floor**: 12,000 SF of retail shell space (3 tenant bays)
- **Floors 2–5**: 48 market-rate residential units (mix of 1BR and 2BR)
- **Floor 6**: Rooftop amenity deck with pool, fitness center, and community room
- **Below grade**: Single-level parking garage (62 spaces)

**Key Team Members at Meridian:**
- **Sarah Chen, AIA** — Principal-in-Charge
- **Marcus Rivera** — Project Manager (your role for this case study)
- **Priya Osei** — Project Architect
- **Two junior staff** — Production team

**Consultants:**
- **StructurePro Engineering** — Structural
- **Greenleaf MEP** — Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing
- **TerraForm Civil** — Civil / Site
- **Lumen Lighting Design** — Specialty lighting (rooftop amenity only)

**Contractor:**
- **Harmon-Wells Construction** — General Contractor (selected via negotiated bid)

### Project Timeline
- **Design start**: January 8, 2024
- **SD completion**: March 15, 2024
- **DD completion**: June 28, 2024
- **CD completion**: October 18, 2024
- **Permit issuance**: December 2, 2024
- **Construction start**: January 6, 2025
- **Substantial Completion target**: February 27, 2026
- **Total construction duration**: 60 weeks

### Current Situation (as of the date of this case study)
It is **June 2025** — roughly 22 weeks into construction. The project is currently
**2 weeks behind schedule** due to unexpected soil contamination discovered during
foundation work, which required a 3-week remediation effort (1 week was recovered
through schedule acceleration). The owner is concerned about the delay and its impact
on pre-leasing commitments for the retail spaces, which have a lease-commencement
date of April 1, 2026.

Several issues have converged this month:
1. The owner wants to add a **green roof system** to part of the 6th-floor amenity deck
   (not in original scope)
2. A **payment dispute** has arisen between the GC and the mechanical subcontractor
3. The **structural engineer's** contract was based on a fixed fee, and they are
   requesting additional compensation for redesign work related to the soil remediation
4. The city has issued a **revised stormwater ordinance** that may affect the site
   civil design

**Article 2 — Architect's Responsibilities**

§ 2.1 The Architect shall provide professional services as described in this Agreement.
The Architect's services consist of those services performed by the Architect, the
Architect's employees, and the Architect's consultants.

§ 2.2 The Architect shall manage the Architect's services, research applicable design
criteria, attend project meetings, communicate with members of the project team, and
report progress to the Owner.

§ 2.3 The Architect shall coordinate its services with those services provided by the
Owner and the Owner's consultants. The Architect shall be entitled to rely on the
accuracy and completeness of services and information furnished by the Owner and the
Owner's consultants.

§ 2.4 **Scope of Basic Services** includes: Schematic Design, Design Development,
Construction Documents, Bidding/Negotiation, and Construction Administration.

§ 2.5 The Architect shall not be responsible for construction means, methods, techniques,
sequences, procedures, or for safety precautions and programs in connection with the Work.

---

**Article 3 — Additional Services**

§ 3.1 Additional Services are not included in Basic Services and, if provided, shall
be compensated in addition to the compensation for Basic Services.

§ 3.2 The Architect shall provide the following Additional Services only if specifically
designated:
- Programming (Not Designated)
- Measured Drawings of Existing Conditions (Not Designated)
- **Sustainability / LEED Consulting (Not Designated)**
- Commissioning (Not Designated)
- **Post-Occupancy Evaluation (Not Designated)**
- Tenant Design Services (Not Designated)
- **Services necessitated by changes in the Owner's program or budget after SD approval
  (Designated — hourly rate per Exhibit B)**

§ 3.3 If services beyond those described in this Agreement are required during
Construction Administration due to **circumstances beyond the Architect's control**,
including but not limited to unforeseen site conditions requiring redesign, the
Architect shall notify the Owner prior to commencing such services. Compensation for
such services shall be as mutually agreed or, if not agreed, at the hourly rates in
Exhibit B.

---

**Article 4 — Owner's Responsibilities**

§ 4.1 The Owner shall provide information in a timely manner regarding requirements
for and limitations on the Project.

§ 4.2 The Owner shall furnish surveys describing physical characteristics, legal
limitations, and utility locations for the site.

§ 4.3 The Owner shall furnish services of geotechnical engineers or other consultants
for subsurface conditions when the Architect requests such information.

§ 4.4 The Owner shall provide prompt written notice to the Architect if the Owner
becomes aware of any fault or defect in the Project.

§ 5.4 Payments are due within **30 days** of invoice date. Interest on unpaid balances

**Article 6 — Dispute Resolution**

§ 6.1 Claims, disputes, or other matters arising out of this Agreement shall first be

   critical. I recommend we schedule a decision meeting no later than June 27.

   overlapping the L4-6 exterior enclosure with late-stage L3 enclosure work

   acceleration costs.

I'll have our fee proposal to you by Thursday.
